# Citation Style

Numbered, IEEE-style, in order of first appearance. In-text: [1], [2], [3].

## Format by source type

**Standard / regulatory guidance**
[N] {Organization}, "{Document title}," {Publication year}. [Online].
Available: {URL}

**Academic paper**
[N] {Author(s)}, "{Paper title}," {Journal/Conference}, vol. X, no. Y,
pp. XX-XX, {Year}. DOI: {doi if available}

**Industry whitepaper / report**
[N] {Organization/Author}, "{Title}," {Publisher}, {Year}. [Online].
Available: {URL}

## Rules
- Every factual claim, statistic, or named practice needs a bracketed
  citation at first mention.
- Prefer primary sources (the standard itself, the regulation itself) over
  articles describing them.
- Never quote more than one short sentence directly from any source; default
  to paraphrase, and always in our own words.
- Dead links get flagged in review, not silently left in.
- References list is alphabetized by organization/author within the numbered
  order established by first appearance — do not resort numerically after
  the fact if it breaks in-text references.
