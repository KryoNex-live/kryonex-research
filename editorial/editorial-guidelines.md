# Editorial Guidelines

## Voice
- Neutral, technical, third-person or descriptive — not promotional.
- Explain trade-offs honestly. Every architecture has limitations; state them
  in the "Limitations" section, not buried in a footnote.
- Prefer precise engineering language over hype ("reduces latency in X
  scenario" not "blazing fast").

## What we don't do
- No claims like "solves," "eliminates," "guarantees" for problems that are
  genuinely hard (traceability, interoperability, etc.) — use "addresses,"
  "mitigates," "reduces."
- No mentions of KryoNex products/services inside the technical body.
- No comparison tables that frame KryoNex favorably against named vendors.
- No unverified statistics — every number needs a source in References.

## Sourcing bar
Minimum source tiers, in order of preference:
1. Standards bodies (GS1, ISO, IEEE) and regulators (FDA, EMA, WHO)
2. Peer-reviewed academic papers
3. Established industry consortia / whitepapers (with named authorship)
4. Reputable trade press (used sparingly, for context only)

Avoid: vendor marketing blogs as primary sources, unattributed blog posts,
anything without a visible author/organization.

## Review gate
No article moves from draft to published without clearing
checklists/review-checklist.md, checklists/seo-checklist.md, and
checklists/ai-discoverability-checklist.md in full.
